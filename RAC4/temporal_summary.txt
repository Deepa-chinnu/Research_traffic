RMSE: Root Mean Squared Error

It measures the square root of the average of squared differences between predicted and actual values.

It gives higher weight to large errors.

Lower RMSE = better model.

MAE: Mean Absolute Error

It measures the average of absolute differences between predicted and actual values.

It treats all errors equally, making it more robust to outliers than RMSE.

Lower MAE = better model.

Both are used to evaluate regression models like the one in your traffic volume prediction work.
